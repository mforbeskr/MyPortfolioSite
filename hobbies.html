Life Outside the Code

When I'm not studying or working on software projects, I enjoy spending my time on activities that keep me both creative and grounded. I believe having interests outside of technology is important — not only for balance, but also for inspiration and long-term motivation.

Gaming has been a long-standing hobby of mine and has shaped the way I think about problem-solving, strategy, and teamwork. Whether it's competitive games or more relaxed experiences, I enjoy the challenge of learning systems, adapting quickly, and working towards shared goals.

I also enjoy programming in my free time, often experimenting with ideas that don't necessarily have a strict goal. These small projects allow me to explore new concepts, improve my understanding of structure and logic, and simply build things for the fun of it.

Staying active is another important part of my life. I enjoy playing basketball and working out, both as a way to stay healthy and to clear my head. Physical activity helps me stay focused and energized — especially during busy periods of study or work.

Music is something I both listen to and create. It's a creative outlet that contrasts nicely with the structured nature of programming, and it plays a big role in how I relax and recharge.

On a more personal level, I value spending time with my family and friends. I live with my partner and our young son, who has added a new and meaningful dimension to everyday life. Balancing family, hobbies, and professional growth is something I care deeply about and actively work towards.
